About Indian Army

Guardians of the Nation

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. With over 1.4 million active personnel, it is the world's second-largest standing army.

The primary mission of the Indian Army is to ensure national security and unity, defend the nation from external aggression and internal threats, and maintain peace and security within its borders.

The Army has a rich history of valour and sacrifice, having participated in several wars and operations, both domestically and internationally, while also contributing significantly to United Nations peacekeeping missions.
